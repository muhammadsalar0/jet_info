Thurster

The Dassault Rafale is powered by two Snecma M88-2 turbofan engines, each producing around 7,500 kgf (75 kN) of thrust with afterburner. These engines are compact, reliable, and designed for high performance across a wide flight envelope, enabling the Rafale to reach speeds up to Mach 1.8. While the M88-2 does not have thrust vectoring like the Su-57, it offers excellent throttle response and fuel efficiency, supporting long-range missions. AIR Brakes

The Dassault Rafale features advanced aerodynamic airbrakes integrated into its design to enhance deceleration and maneuvering. The primary airbrake is located on the upper fuselage behind the cockpit, and additional braking is assisted by the aircraft's canards and control surfaces. These elements work together to rapidly reduce speed during landing approaches or combat maneuvers without compromising stability. The Rafale's airbrake system is seamlessly managed by the flight control computer, allowing precise, automatic deployment to maintain agility and control during high-speed operations
