Thurster

The JF-17 Thunder is powered by the Klimov RD-93 afterburning turbofan engine, a variant of the Russian RD-33 used in the MiG-29. This engine produces around 18,000 to 19,000 pounds of thrust with afterburner, enabling the JF-17 to achieve supersonic speeds and agile maneuverability. The RD-93 features a modular design for easier maintenance and includes an afterburner section for increased thrust during combat or high-speed flight. While not as powerful as engines in larger fighters, it provides a good balance of performance, reliability, and cost for a lightweight multirole aircraft like the JF-17.

AIR Brakes

The JF-17 Thunder is equipped with a single air brake located on the underside of the fuselage, just behind the main landing gear. This hydraulically operated air brake helps reduce the aircraft’s speed during descent, landing, or combat maneuvers. By increasing aerodynamic drag, it allows for quicker deceleration without significantly affecting stability. Its simple and robust design complements the JF-17’s lightweight and cost-effective structure, enhancing operational control during various flight phases.
