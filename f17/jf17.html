Thruster

The JF-17 Thunder, also known as the FC-1 Xiaolong in China, is a lightweight, multi-role combat aircraft developed jointly by Pakistan and China. One of its key components is the engine, or thruster, which powers the jet and provides it with high agility and speed. The JF-17 is primarily equipped with the Russian-designed Klimov RD-93 turbofan engine, a variant of the RD-33 used in the MiG-29. This engine delivers a thrust of about 49.4 kN dry and 84.4 kN with afterburner, enabling the JF-17 to reach speeds up to Mach 1.6. Efforts have also been made to explore Chinese alternatives like the WS-13 engine for future variants

Air Brakes

The JF-17 Thunder is equipped with air brakes designed to enhance its maneuverability and control during high-speed flight and landing. These air brakes are located on the sides of the fuselage, just behind the cockpit area. When deployed, they increase drag, allowing the aircraft to decelerate rapidly without relying solely on engine thrust reduction or aerodynamic surfaces. This feature is especially useful during combat maneuvers and landing approaches, giving pilots better control over speed and positioning. The air brakes are hydraulically operated and integrated into the flight control system for efficient and responsive handling.
