Thruster

The F-35 fighter jet’s thrust comes from its advanced Pratt & Whitney F135 engine, which delivers over 40,000 pounds of thrust, enabling supersonic speeds and exceptional maneuverability. Its afterburner boosts power for rapid acceleration, while specialized nozzle designs enhance stealth by reducing heat and radar signatures. In the F-35B variant, a unique swiveling rear nozzle and lift fan allow vertical takeoff and landing, giving the jet unmatched operational flexibility.

Air Brakes

The F-35 does not use traditional air brakes like older fighter jets; instead, it relies on a combination of control surfaces—such as its flaperons, rudder, and tail surfaces—to create drag and slow down. By adjusting these surfaces in opposite directions, the aircraft can decelerate efficiently without adding large external panels that would affect stealth. This integrated braking system helps maintain the F-35’s low radar signature while still allowing precise speed control during landing and combat maneuvers.
