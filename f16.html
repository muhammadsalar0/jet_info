Thurster

The F-16 Fighting Falcon is equipped with a Pratt & Whitney F100 or General Electric F110 afterburning turbofan engine, both of which feature a variable-area convergent-divergent (CD) nozzle known as a thrust vectoring nozzle. This engine produces up to 29,000 pounds of thrust with afterburner, enabling the F-16’s high speed, agility, and combat performance. The engine includes a digital electronic control system for precise management of thrust, fuel flow, and engine parameters. Its compact and efficient design contributes to the F-16’s reputation as a highly maneuverable multirole fighter

AIR Brakes

The F-16 Fighting Falcon is equipped with a split-type air brake located on the upper fuselage just behind the cockpit. This air brake consists of two panels that open upward and downward to create drag and help decelerate the aircraft rapidly during flight or landing. Controlled hydraulically, the air brake enhances maneuverability by allowing quick speed reduction, especially during combat or approach phases. Its design minimizes impact on the aircraft’s aerodynamic stability, maintaining control even during aggressive braking
