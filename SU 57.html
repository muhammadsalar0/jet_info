The Su-57 is powered by two advanced turbofan engines, currently the Saturn AL-41F1 with plans to upgrade to the Izdeliye 30 engine in future production models. These thrust-vectoring engines provide exceptional maneuverability, allowing the Su-57 to perform extreme aerobatic maneuvers and maintain control at high angles of attack. The engines produce around 14,500 kgf (142 kN) of thrust each with afterburners and feature 3D thrust vectoring nozzles, which significantly enhance the aircraft's agility in dogfights while also contributing to stealth through flat nozzle design in future variants

AIR Brakes

The Su-57 is equipped with advanced airbrakes integrated into its wing and fuselage design. These airbrakes help reduce speed rapidly during high-speed flight, maneuvers, and landing approaches. Unlike older jets with large, visible panels, the Su-57’s airbrakes are designed to preserve stealth by being flush with the body when not in use. Their placement and operation work in coordination with the aircraft’s flight control system to enhance aerodynamic braking without compromising radar signature or maneuverability.
